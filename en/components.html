Self‑Undermining Dynamics

A Structural Model of Open Political Systems

Structural Components

The structural model identifies core components that drive political systems toward self‑undermining behavior. These include:

  • Recursive feedback loops – mechanisms that amplify internal dynamics.
  • Structural asymmetries – imbalances that accumulate over time.
  • Systemic amplification – processes that intensify political signals.
  • Adaptive destabilization – shifts that weaken long‑term stability.

Together, these components form the backbone of the model and explain how open systems evolve toward instability through their own internal logic.
