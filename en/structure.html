Structural Components

The structural layer of SUD consists of three core elements that interact to produce long‑term destabilization.

1. Recursive Feedback

Actions taken by agents modify the system, which then alters the incentives that shape future actions. This creates loops that amplify instability.

2. Destabilizing Incentives

Systems often reward behaviors that undermine long‑term stability, such as short‑term political gains, information manipulation, or strategic exploitation.

3. Adaptive Agents

Agents respond strategically to shifting constraints, often accelerating destabilizing processes when they perceive advantages.
