Self‑Undermining Dynamics

A Structural Model of Open Political Systems

Meta Layer

Beyond structural components, political systems undergo meta‑level transformations. These shifts alter the system’s logic, its internal rules, and the way it interprets political signals.

Meta dynamics include:

  • Meta‑stability – stability generated at higher structural layers.
  • Meta‑feedback – recursive loops that operate above the system’s base level.
  • Meta‑transformation – changes that redefine the system’s identity.

These processes explain how political systems evolve not only within their structure, but also beyond it, creating new forms of self‑undermining behavior.
